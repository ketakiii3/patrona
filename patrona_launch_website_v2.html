how it started

We asked women what the walk home
actually feels like. They told us everything.

"I hold my keys like a weapon. Every single night."

Before we wrote a single line of code, we sat down with women — students, late-shift workers, people who'd been on that walk. What they described wasn't just fear of something happening. It was the ordinary, exhausting anxiety of every single time.

Patrona came from those conversations.

how it works

Start a walk. Talk, or don't.
Get home safe.

1

Tap once to start your walk

Patrona picks up like a friend calling to check on you. Your GPS turns on quietly. Your people are ready if they're ever needed.

2

Talk, or just let the company settle you

Chat about your day, or stay quiet. If you go silent for a while, Patrona gently checks in. Hands-free. Eyes up. No phone-staring required.

3

Say the word — Patrona acts

A natural signal tells Patrona something's wrong — no dramatic button, nothing that tips anyone off. It alerts your contacts and gives you one tap to 911 with your location already loaded.

4

"I'm home."

Check in when you arrive. Your contacts get the all-clear. The walk is closed.

why patrona

Not a panic button.
A presence.

Every other safety app waits for you to decide you're in danger. Patrona is with you before that moment — so it hopefully never comes.

◎

A real voice on the line

Two-way conversation, not a recording. A friend's voice, not a script. Hands-free, eyes on your surroundings.

~

Silence detection

Go quiet and Patrona gently checks in — so a problem is caught even when you can't say it out loud.

◐

Covert safe word

A casual word or phrase — nothing dramatic — tells Patrona to act without tipping off anyone near you.

⊕

Escalation with context

One tap to 911. Your live location and call context already loaded. Your contacts already notified.

◷

Looks normal

Being on a call reads as occupied and connected — not alone, not scared, not an easy target.

✓

"I'm home safe"

Live GPS the whole walk. A simple check-in closes the loop for you and the people who care about you.

who we are

Two Columbia grad students
who'd been on that walk.

[ photo — Ketaki ]
[ photo — Sahiti ]

We're Ketaki and Sahiti. We built Patrona because we were tired of texting three people every time we walked home at night — and because everyone around us was doing the same thing and calling it fine.

We started with interviews, not code. We wanted to understand what the walk actually feels like before we tried to change it. Then we built, entered the Columbia AI for Good Hackathon, and won. We kept going because the problem didn't stop mattering.
